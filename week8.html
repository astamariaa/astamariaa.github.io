Shifting Shadows: How Crime in San Francisco Has Changed Over Time

A magazine-style data story exploring how crime patterns in San Francisco have evolved over the past decade.

Understanding the Dataset

This project uses the San Francisco Police Department’s publicly available crime dataset (2010–2020). It includes reports of various incidents, along with timestamps, coordinates, and categories. For clarity, we removed duplicate records and filtered out prostitution-related cases (covered in prior coursework).

Crime Trends Over Time

This time-series chart shows how crime volume fluctuated over the decade. A notable dip appears in 2020, likely reflecting COVID-19 lockdown effects.

Fig. 1. Reported crimes by year (2010–2020).

Hotspots of Crime

Mapping incident data reveals spatial clusters. The Mission District and parts of downtown SF remain high-density zones for various types of crime.

Fig. 2. Crime heatmap over San Francisco.

Insights

  • Property crime remains the most prevalent across all years.
  • 2020 had an unusually sharp drop in total incidents.
  • Some neighborhoods consistently report higher levels of violent crime.

Conclusion

San Francisco’s crime patterns shift over time and space, reflecting larger social and economic factors. Visualizing this data reveals not just numbers, but stories — spikes, slow declines, and persistent disparities across the city.
